In 2025 more than 30% of data will be real time data !

The revolution of connected object paves the demand for efficient stream processing technologies. Logisland is one of them: it reliently capture, analyse and compress huge volumes of very tiny to very large data. Logisland is not an ETL, this is much beyond: it Captures, Compresses, Analyses, Loads. This is an extendable framework that already integrates the best of the best of stream analytics and storage through pluggins.

Your one stop shop framework for all streaming jobs

Real-time distributed stream processing is one area where many different approaches to data storage, management, and analysis coexist or conflict. Depending on your use case, you may wish to keep track of event sequences with Kafka or you will have a vital need for the speed but ephemeral processing of events with MQTT. You may need temporal windowed analysis with Spark Streaming to provide context for your decisions or want a reptilian reaction to events with Kafka streams, Flink or pure Java. Logisland is the only technology that offers a unified approach to stream analytics with simple changes in the configuration of message buses and distributed engines. A guarantee of independence and stability in a fast changing technological world.

Pluggins for major stream analysis use cases

Everybody loves Google for providing Web Analytics for your Web sites. With Logisland though, in parallel, you can route the same click streams to your own analytics. A bunch of pluggins and tools will give you access to your streams and you will be able to link them to your back office for better recommandations, ads or simply a greater understanding of your business and customers (vision 360 degree).

IoT events are the fastest growing data sets. Many companies find incredible use cases using sensors and actuators. This data of course must be captured, stored, analysed. Logisland provides tools for advanced IoT data compaction for storage in the major time series databases, including the one proposed by Hurence, based on SolR. Pluggins for smart alerting, prediction and trends spotting are also available.
